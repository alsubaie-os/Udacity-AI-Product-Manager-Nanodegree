Medical Image Data Annotation Job

Instructions

Overview

In this job, you will be presented with chest X-ray images to determine if pneumonia is presented in the images provided or not.

The most important areas to pay attention to are the lungs and the diaphragm.

A normal, healthy image will depict clear lungs without any areas of abnormal cloudiness/opacity; there may be structured, web-like vasculature in the lungs otherwise that area should be clear. In healthy images, you are also more likely to see a diaphragm shadow.

Example of a normal, healthy image:

A pneumonia image may include a few things:
•Areas of cloudiness/opacity in several concentrated areas or one large area.
•General pattern of opacity that obscures the structure of the lungs, heart, and diaphragm.

Example of a pneumonia image: (Left) a concentrated, opaque area in the lungs, (Right) multiple, smaller opaque areas throughout the lung area, and any diaphragm shadow is obscured)

Steps

  1. Examine the image.
  2. If there is no Pneumonia in the image, click No and continue with the following image.
  3. If there is Pneumonia in the image, click Yesand check the appropriate symptom/s for the enabled question, and continue with the following image.
  4. If you are not sure of Pneumonia, click Not Sureand rate how likely this image shows Pneumonia for the enabled question, and continue with the following image.

Rules Tips

Rules:

  • To determine if there is Pneumonia in the image only look for opaqueness and/or diaphragm shadow as explained in the Overview section.
  • If there are multiple symptoms in the image, you can check multiple boxes in the symptoms question.
  • The rating scale 1 represents the Not Sure option corresponds to Not likely, and 5corresponds to Extremely likely.

Tips:

  • Please be advised that within the lungs there may be a concentrated opaque area/s in the lungs or smaller opaque areas throughout the lung area. This could make it difficult to visualize the diaphragm's shadow.
